Flanking

Flanking

Take an off-angle (not main choke) to pressure supports and force enemy cooldowns. Your goal isn’t always a kill — it’s to split attention so your team wins the front fight.

  • Wait for your tank to engage before you commit.
  • Use cover and escapes; don’t flank with no exit plan.
  • Prioritize isolated supports/DPS over shooting the tank.
  • Disengage as soon as key cooldowns are used (Recall, Wraith, Dash, etc.).

Sniping

Sniping

Control sightlines and win fights with first picks. Sniping is about positioning, timing, and discipline — not just aim.

  • Hold high ground when possible; it improves sightlines and safety.
  • Change angles after a kill/whiff so you don’t get dove.
  • Don’t tunnel vision: watch for flankers and listen for footsteps.
  • Play around shields/cover: peek, shoot, unpeek.

Burst Damage

Burst Damage

Burst is best when you coordinate timing: force a target to use a defensive cooldown, then finish the kill before healing catches up.

  • Focus the same target as your team (call or ping).
  • Look for enemies without mobility or cover.
  • Save burst combos for when the enemy is committed (after they walk in).
  • Don’t waste burst on tanks unless they’re truly out of position.

High Ground Control

High Ground Control

High ground gives better angles, safer damage, and makes it harder for enemies to contest. Winning high ground often wins the entire fight.

  • Take high ground early (before the fight starts).
  • Drop only when you can secure a kill or you’re forced off.
  • Use mobility to rotate between ledges instead of standing still.
  • Examples: Soldier: 76, Ashe, Sojourn, Hanzo.

Poke / Spam Pressure

Poke / Spam Pressure

Constant damage builds ultimate charge, forces supports to heal, and punishes slow rotations.

  • Hold angles where enemies must cross open space.
  • Track enemy healing.
  • Back up as they close distance instead of brawling.
  • Examples: Pharah, Junkrat, Hanzo, Ashe.
